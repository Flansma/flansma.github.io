I am a Master's student at the Graduate School of Medicine, Kyoto University, working in the Okuno Laboratory on AI-driven drug discovery with the supercomputer "Fugaku."

During my undergraduate studies at Kwansei Gakuin University, I built a foundation in life sciences, computational science, and programming, developing core skills in bioinformatics.

My current research focuses on developing transformer-based language models for medium-sized peptide property prediction, bridging the gap between small-molecule and protein informatics. I aim to accelerate the drug discovery pipeline by building AI tools that enable more efficient and accurate prediction of peptide drug properties.
